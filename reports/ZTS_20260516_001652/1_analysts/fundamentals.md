FINAL TRANSACTION PROPOSAL: **BUY**

## ZTS Fundamental Report as of 2026-05-16

### Executive summary
ZTS (Zoetis Inc.) remains a high-quality, cash-generative animal health business with strong margins, steady earnings growth, and a shareholder-friendly capital return policy. The latest reported quarter was softer on operating cash flow and free cash flow, but core profitability held up well. Valuation looks reasonable on earnings and EBITDA, while leverage is elevated but still manageable.

**Bottom line:** ZTS looks attractive for long-term investors who want a defensive healthcare name with recurring demand, a dividend, and buybacks. The main caution is that capital allocation has been aggressive, with buybacks funded partly by debt, which has pushed leverage higher.

---

## Company profile
**Zoetis Inc.** operates in the **Healthcare** sector, within **Drug Manufacturers - Specialty & Generic**. ZTS is best known as the global leader in animal health, serving both **companion animals** and **livestock** with medicines, vaccines, diagnostics, and related products.

Why this matters:
- Animal health demand is typically more resilient than many cyclical industries.
- The business benefits from recurring spend, pricing power, and a broad installed base of products.
- Product portfolios in companion animals tend to be relatively sticky, supporting margins and cash flow.

- **Debt-to-equity:** very high on a reported basis because equity is compressed by buybacks and intangible-heavy assets
- **Net debt / EBITDA:** roughly **1.7x**, which is manageable
- **Interest coverage:** very comfortable, based on annual EBIT vs. interest expense

### What stands out

3. **Tangible book is negative.** This is common in acquisitive, intangible-rich businesses, but it means book value is not a useful valuation anchor.
4. **Equity has shrunk materially** because of share repurchases. That helps EPS, but it also raises leverage ratios.

## Key risks
1. **Slower growth:** Revenue growth is only low single digits.
2. **Leverage:** Debt has risen meaningfully, largely alongside buybacks.
3. **Cash flow volatility:** Latest-quarter FCF was weaker than prior periods.
4. **Working capital pressure:** Inventory and receivables deserve monitoring.
5. **Book value is not a useful floor:** Negative tangible book means valuation should be based on earnings and cash flow, not P/B.

## Trading takeaway
ZTS looks like a **quality defensive BUY on fundamental grounds**, especially for investors seeking:
- stable profitability,
- a recurring-demand business,
- a dividend,
- and buyback-driven EPS growth.

The main discipline point is to **avoid overpaying if the stock rallies back to premium levels**, because the current appeal comes from a combination of solid fundamentals and moderate valuation. Near term, watch for:
